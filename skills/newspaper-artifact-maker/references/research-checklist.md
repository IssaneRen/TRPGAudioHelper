# Research Checklist

Use this checklist before rendering the final prop.

## Required Evidence

- Real publication: founded before the issue date and active in the target city or region.
- Issue date: plausible publication day. If exact issue availability is unknown, state that the prop is a reconstruction.
- Real news fillers: each item has a date close to the issue and a source URL.
- Fictional hook: based on the scenario brief or user-provided lore, not presented as a verified historical event in notes.
- Geographic logic: events appearing in a London paper should be plausible for London readers, such as empire news, international diplomacy, shipping, theatre, markets, or domestic politics.

## Recommended Sources

- Newspaper archives or library catalogues for mastheads and publication dates.
- Hansard, government records, and treaty collections for parliamentary and diplomatic events.
- Encyclopaedia entries are acceptable for orientation, but cross-check important facts where possible.
- Existing project wiki entries can support scenario/lore content, but not real-world claims.

## Review Questions

- Are any filler stories invented while sounding factual?
- Does any fictional scenario line accidentally spoil Keeper-only material?
- Does the visual hierarchy point to the intended handout clue?
- Is the date on the masthead compatible with the dated facts?
- Are all visible words legible at the requested image size?
